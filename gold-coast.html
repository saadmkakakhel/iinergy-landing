What you actually get

Benefits of solar + battery.

Six reasons the numbers stack up. Every one is a dollar figure or a fact — no green fluff.

Save money on power

Stop selling your solar at 5c/kWh and buying it back at 30–35c. A battery keeps every kilowatt your panels make on your side of the meter. Bill drops the day we switch it on.

Get paid for excess

Whatever your battery can't store, the grid still buys. Feed-in tariffs put real dollars back when your panels overproduce on a summer afternoon — your meter runs the other way.

Add value to the property

A solar + battery setup is a hard asset on the property's value sheet. Appraisers price it in. Buyers price it in. You spend it once, you get it back at sale.

Stay on when the grid drops

Storms, faults, brownouts — your battery flips the house to off-grid mode the second the grid wobbles. Fridge, lights, Wi-Fi, fans stay live. You won't even notice it switched.

Low maintenance

Set it, leave it. Tier 1 panels, 25-year warranty, self-monitoring inverter that pings us before you notice. If anything ever does need servicing, our in-house crew handles it.

VPP-ready & rebate-locked

Every battery we install qualifies for the federal rebate and is VPP-ready out of the box. You get the discount up-front, plus the option to earn from a Virtual Power Plant later.

The catch nobody mentioned

Right now, your solar is making someone else money.

Reckon solar's a one-and-done deal? Here's what nobody told you when they put panels on your roof.

  • i.
    Without a battery, you sell power at around 5c/kWh during the day — and buy it back at 30–35c/kWh at night. Same power. Six times the price.
  • ii.
    Even with solar on the roof, you're still renting your house from the power companies. Once the sun drops, you're back on the grid.
  • iii.
    A battery flips it. Your solar stays yours. Cheap power during the day, your power at night. Grid goes down — you stay on.
